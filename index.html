Why Porto?

Porto, the city of coastal beauty

Porto, one of Portugal's oldest cities, is renowned for its stunning architecture, rich history, and vibrant culture. Nestled along the Douro River, it boasts a picturesque riverside area, known as the Ribeira, where colorful buildings and lively cafés create a welcoming atmosphere. The city's iconic landmarks, such as the Dom Luís I Bridge and the historic Livraria Lello bookstore, attract visitors from around the world. Porto is also famous for its wine production, particularly Port wine, which has been a significant part of the city's economy and identity for centuries.

Beyond its scenic beauty, Porto is a hub of creativity and innovation. The city is home to a thriving arts scene, with numerous galleries, theaters, and music venues that reflect its dynamic spirit. Local cuisine is another highlight, featuring delicious dishes like bacalhau à Gomes de Sá and the beloved francesinha sandwich. With its blend of tradition and modernity, Porto offers a unique experience for residents and visitors alike, making it a truly special place to call home.

CA Downtown - Brunch, Coffee e Matcha

CA Downtown - Brunch, Coffee e Matcha

Praça de Carlos Alberto 89, 4050-158 Porto

CA Downtown is the perfect spot for a relaxed and high-quality brunch experience. Cervejaria Gulden Draak Bierhuis - Porto

Cervejaria Gulden Draak Bierhuis - Porto

R. de José Falcão N. 82, 4050-315 Porto

Cervejaria Gulden Draak Bierhuis is a must-visit spot in Porto for those who love Belgian beer and hearty, traditional cuisine. Known for its wide selection of Belgian brews, this charming brewery offers an authentic experience, with a cozy, welcoming atmosphere. Pair your beer with delicious local dishes, from savory stews to fresh seafood, all served with the signature warmth of Portuguese hospitality. It's a perfect place to relax, enjoy great food, and discover some unique beers from Belgium.
